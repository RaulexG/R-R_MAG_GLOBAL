Techniques to study

by Ricardo · Published May 2025

Grammar focus: This article uses First Conditional to explain study techniques.

Do you want to study better? There are study techniques that help you learn more. They are tools that help you remember information, focus better, and feel more motivated to study.

Not all techniques work the same for everyone, so if you try them, you will know which one works best for you. These techniques can help you at school or university.

Study Tips

  • If you study for 20 minutes, you will take a 5-minute break. This is called the Pomodoro method. If you take a break, you will feel less stress. If you do 4 study cycles, you will take a 30-minute break.
  • If you use a mind map, you will find information faster. A mind map shows ideas, words, or pictures about one main topic.
  • If you write a summary, you will include the most important information. First, you need to read and understand the text. Then, you separate the main idea. If you do this, you will pass your exams.
  • If you compare concepts, you will use a comparison chart. If you use this, you will see the differences and similarities clearly.
  • If you do a self-assessment, you will check what you have learned. If you ask questions, you will find the important information. This will help your learning.

If you keep using these techniques, you will learn more for your next tests or projects.
