Natural Treasures of the World

by Raul · Published May 2025

Grammar focus: This article uses Simple Present to describe facts, and Simple Past to tell stories from the past.

All around the world, nature creates places and events that are beautiful, powerful, and full of wonder. These treasures are not made by people—they are made by water, wind, fire, and time.

“They remind us how amazing and strong our planet is.”

One famous place is the Grand Canyon in the United States. It is very big and very deep. A river made it over millions of years. People see red and orange rocks that look like a painting. Many visitors take photos and walk on the trails.

In the far north, we can see the Northern Lights. This is a natural light show in the sky. It happens in places like Norway, Canada, and Iceland. The lights are green, purple, and pink. They move slowly across the night sky. People watch quietly while the sky shines. Long ago, some cultures thought the lights were messages from the gods.

In South America, we find Iguazu Falls. These are big waterfalls between Brazil and Argentina. The water falls with great power. Sometimes, you can see rainbows. People feel the force of nature and hear the loud sound of the water. The area is full of green forests and animals.

A World of Wonders

These are just a few of the world’s natural wonders. From high mountains to deep canyons, from shining skies to strong waterfalls, nature gives us beauty and inspiration. These special places show us that Earth is full of magic. And we must protect it.
